## Rohit is 25 and just graduated from MBA college. Earning ₹90,000 per month he has two SIPs running, some money in stocks, a fixed deposit, and around ₹2 lakh in his savings account. He feels responsible. He feels like he is “doing investing.”

So when I asked him – how much is your total net worth?  
Answer: he did not know the exact amount.

And that’s the same situation where many young investors find themselves today.

## Main problem what you as an investor make

When we think of wealth, we think of it as a number telling our bank balance or our investment portfolio.

But this is wrong, your real financial position is a combination of everything:

- savings  
- investments  
- deposits  
- assets  
- minus loans and liabilities  

---

The main concern is not that people are not saving or investing much. In fact, many students, MBA aspirants, and early professionals have already started SIPs and started investing.

The issue is that their money is spread across multiple platforms, and none of the platforms gives them the overall picture at one place.

So even responsible and disciplined investors like you struggle to answer:

**Am I actually financially on track?**

And if you are clear with this, your financial decisions become more viable and clear. But even people who want to track their finances try spreadsheets or notes. Consistency becomes difficult. Over time, tracking stops and things start becoming cluttered.

So we need a structured system.

Let us again continue with the same example of Rohit who earns ₹90,000 per month.

### He has:

| Category            | Amount (₹) |
|---------------------|------------|
| Savings             | 2,00,000   |
| Mutual Funds (SIP)  | 1,00,000   |
| Stocks              | 50,000     |
| **Total Assets**    | **3,50,000** |
| Education Loan      | 3,00,000   |
| **Net Position**    | **50,000** |

If he only looks at his investments and savings, he feels confident. But when everything is combined:

- Total assets = ₹3.5 lakh  
- Loan = ₹3 lakh  
- Net position = ₹50,000  

This doesn’t mean he’s doing bad. It just means he needs clarity to plan the next steps. When you see everything together, you make better decisions:

- how much to invest  
- how much to repay  
- how much to save
